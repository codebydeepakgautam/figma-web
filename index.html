Level Up Your Child's Vocabulary

 Megha Sharma   02 Dec, 2024

Dictionary keepers were the gatekeepers of vocabulary back in our day. The war cry for
any unknown term was "look it up!" But let's be honest, those definitions often left the
impression that they were written by…. well, dictionary writers (no offense to
lexicographers out there!).

The fact is, at any age, having a strong vocabulary is equivalent to having superpowers.
However, for a child's educational journey, traditional dictionaries can be like a child's
kryptonite. They frequently use difficult terminology and don't provide compelling age
-optimized explanations that really connect with young readers. Sadly, they have
same example, context for all ages, all cultures, all situations; they make words, words,
and not stories, easy to remember and use.

That's where age-appropriate definitions come in handy, as shown by Beyond
Dictionary. Our tool personalises explanations based on your child's individual learning
stage, in contrast to standard dictionaries.

Confused? Let’s see what we mean:

Do you remember that old, worn dictionary on the shelf that claimed to hold the keys
to understanding the English language? Well, its definitions can be as thrilling as
watching paint dry for a lot of us parents. Suppose if you have to search or explain
simple meaning of ‘Enthusiasm’ to your child, the conventional dictionaries would give
you result like

Enthusiasm (noun):
1 Absorbing or controlling possession of the mind by any interest or pursuit; lively
   interest.
2 An occupation, activity, or pursuit in which such interest is shown.
3 (archaic) Any of various forms of extreme religious devotion. 

See the problem? This definition might leave a 5-year-old scratching their head and a
12-year-old reaching for their phone (because, let's face it, who uses the word "archaic"
anymore?).
Here's how Beyond Dictionary breaks down "enthusiasm" for different age groups:

Enthusiasm (For 5-year-olds):
It means being super excited and happy about something. Like when you feel so happy
about playing with your favorite toy or going to the park with friends that you can't
smiling and laughing!

Enthusiasm (For 12-year-olds):
It's a strong feeling of excitement and interest in something you love or are passionate
about. It's about having lots of energy and positivity towards things you enjoy. Think
about a hobby you love, like playing a sport or creating art. When you have enthusiasm
for it, you get inspired and motivated to keep practicing and getting better!

The difference is clear: Complex words become simpler with the help of Beyond
Dictionary. It makes use of relatable examples, lively language, and explanations that
are age-appropriate in place of boring terminology. It's what separates an enjoyable
educational experience from a boring lecture!  

Imagine now that your child greets every new word with delight rather than fear. That
is Beyond Dictionary's strength!
